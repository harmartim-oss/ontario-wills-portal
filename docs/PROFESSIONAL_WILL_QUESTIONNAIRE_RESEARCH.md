# Professional Will Questionnaire Research - Ontario Law Firms

## Overview
This document compiles research findings from professional Ontario law firm will intake forms and questionnaires to inform the development of a comprehensive, robust will creation system.

## Key Sources Reviewed
1. **MAP Law - Will Intake Form for Wills and POAs Personal** (2015)
2. **WOW Law - Will Intake Questionnaire** (Toronto Lawyer)
3. **Oakville Family Lawyer - Will Intake Form**

---

## Core Questionnaire Sections Identified

### Part 1: Personal Information & Urgency
- **Urgency Assessment**: Is there any reason for urgency in will preparation?
- **Identification Requirements**: Two pieces of ID (at least one with picture)
- **Full Legal Name**: As it appears on government documents
- **Place of Birth**: For identification purposes
- **Date of Birth**: For age and capacity verification
- **Citizenship/SIN**: For tax and identification purposes
- **Residence Address**: Current and any future residence
- **Contact Information**: Phone, email, cell phone
- **Occupation & Employer**: For income and asset context
- **Other Known Names**: Aliases or maiden names

### Part 2: Marital Status & Relationships
- **Current Marital Status**: Single, Common Law, Engaged, Married, Separated, Divorced, Widowed
- **Marriage Contracts**: Cohabitation Agreement, Marriage Contract, Separation Agreement, Court Orders
- **Previous Marriages**: Details of prior marriages and obligations
- **Spouse Information**: Name, address, date of marriage, marital property
- **Spouse's Obligations**: Continuing support obligations, pension obligations
- **Spouse's Previous Marriages**: Any obligations from prior relationships

### Part 3: Children & Grandchildren
- **Number of Children**: Count and details
- **For Each Child**:
  - Full legal name
  - Date of birth
  - Current address
  - Marital status
  - Children (grandchildren of testator)
  - Any special circumstances (disability, financial issues, substance abuse)
  - Relationship quality (estranged, close, etc.)
  
- **Grandchildren Information**: If applicable
- **Dependent Children**: Any children under 18 or dependent adults

### Part 4: Guardianship & Custody
- **Minor Children Guardians**: Who should have custody if testator dies before children reach age of majority
- **Alternate Guardians**: Backup guardians if primary cannot serve
- **Guardian Qualifications**: Age, stability, values alignment
- **Guardianship Preferences**: Joint guardianship vs. sole guardianship
- **Testamentary Guardianship**: Appointment of guardian for minor children

### Part 5: Assets - Financial
- **Bank Accounts**: Institution, account type, balance, ownership
- **GICs (Guaranteed Investment Certificates)**: Amount, maturity date, institution
- **Stocks**: Company, number of shares, current value
- **Bonds**: Type, issuer, face value, current value
- **Debentures**: Details and value
- **Mutual Funds**: Fund name, number of units, value
- **Investment Accounts**: Brokerage, account type, value
- **RRSPs (Registered Retirement Savings Plans)**: Institution, value, beneficiary designation
- **RRIFs (Registered Retirement Income Funds)**: Institution, value, beneficiary designation
- **TFSAs (Tax-Free Savings Accounts)**: Institution, value, beneficiary designation
- **Other Savings**: Savings bonds, money market accounts, etc.
- **Life Insurance**: Policy numbers, face value, beneficiary designations
- **Pension Plans**: Employer pension, value, beneficiary options

### Part 6: Assets - Real Property
- **Primary Residence**: Address, ownership structure, mortgage details, date acquired, estimated value
- **Secondary Properties**: Cottages, investment properties, rental properties
- **For Each Property**:
  - Full legal address
  - Registered owners
  - Mortgage details (lender, balance, interest rate)
  - Date acquired
  - Estimated current value
  - Tenancy type (joint tenancy vs. tenancy in common)
  - Any liens or encumbrances

### Part 7: Assets - Personal Property
- **Vehicles**: Make, model, year, value, ownership
- **Boats**: Type, value, ownership
- **Jewellery**: Description, estimated value, sentimental value
- **Artwork**: Description, artist, estimated value
- **Valuable Collectables**: Coins, stamps, memorabilia, etc.
- **Household Furnishings**: Valuable items, heirlooms
- **Digital Assets**: Computers, software, online accounts
- **Firearms**: Type, registration, value

### Part 8: Business Interests
- **Business Name**: Legal name and operating name
- **Business Address**: Location
- **Ownership Structure**: 
  - Sole Proprietor
  - Partnership
  - Corporation
  - LLC/Other
- **Shareholder Agreement**: Existence and terms
- **Partnership Agreement**: Existence and terms
- **Percentage Ownership**: % owned by testator
- **Business Value**: Estimated value or valuation method
- **Buy-Sell Agreements**: Any agreements with other owners
- **Key Person Insurance**: Life insurance on testator for business
- **Employees**: Number and key personnel
- **Succession Plan**: Who should take over business

### Part 9: Liabilities & Debts
- **Mortgages**: Lender, balance, interest rate, property
- **Lines of Credit**: Lender, balance, interest rate
- **Credit Card Debt**: Creditors, balances
- **Personal Loans**: Lender, balance, terms
- **Business Loans**: Lender, balance, terms
- **Taxes Owing**: Any outstanding tax obligations
- **Other Debts**: Any other liabilities

### Part 10: Executors & Trustees
- **Primary Executor/Trustee**:
  - Full name
  - Address
  - Relationship to testator
  - Age and capacity
  - Willingness to serve
  - Professional qualifications (lawyer, accountant, etc.)
  
- **Alternate Executor/Trustee**: Same information as primary
- **Multiple Executors**: 
  - Joint (must act together) vs. Jointly and Severally (can act independently)
  - How to handle disagreements
  
- **Trustee Powers**: 
  - Investment powers
  - Distribution powers
  - Discretionary vs. mandatory distributions
  - Compensation/fees

### Part 11: Beneficiaries - Specific Gifts
- **Specific Bequests**: 
  - Item or amount
  - Recipient name and relationship
  - Alternate beneficiary if primary predeceases
  - Conditions or restrictions
  
- **Examples**:
  - "My red convertible to my son John"
  - "$50,000 to my daughter Jane"
  - "My engagement ring to my granddaughter"

### Part 12: Beneficiaries - Residue
- **Residual Estate Distribution**: Everything remaining after debts, taxes, and specific gifts
- **Distribution Method**: 
  - Percentages (e.g., 50% to child A, 50% to child B)
  - Per stirpes (by bloodline) vs. per capita (equal shares)
  - Outright vs. in trust
  
- **Alternate Beneficiaries**: If primary beneficiary predeceases
- **Charitable Gifts**: Any donations to charities or foundations

### Part 13: Trusts & Special Provisions
- **Testamentary Trusts**: Trusts created by the will
- **Henson Trusts**: For beneficiaries with disabilities (ODSP protection)
- **Age Contingencies**: "Receive at age 25, 30, 35, etc."
- **Conditions on Gifts**: "Only if beneficiary graduates university"
- **Restrictions**: "Not to be used for alcohol or drugs"
- **Spendthrift Provisions**: Protection from creditors
- **Powers of Appointment**: Allowing beneficiary to redirect gifts

### Part 14: Guardianship for Dependant Adults
- **Adult Dependants**: Any adult children with disabilities or special needs
- **Guardian Appointment**: Who should manage their affairs
- **Trustee for Special Needs**: Who manages funds for their benefit
- **Henson Trust Considerations**: For ODSP-eligible dependants

### Part 15: Existing Documents
- **Existing Will**: Copies provided, revocation of prior wills
- **Power of Attorney for Property**: Existing POA details
- **Power of Attorney for Personal Care**: Existing POA details
- **Marriage/Cohabitation Agreements**: Copies provided
- **Separation Agreements**: Copies provided
- **Court Orders**: Any relevant court orders

### Part 16: Capacity & Special Circumstances
- **Capacity Assessment**: Any capacity-related diagnosis or concerns
- **Mental Health**: Any relevant mental health history
- **Substance Abuse**: Any history of substance abuse
- **Family Conflict**: Any anticipated family disputes or challenges
- **Estrangement**: Any estranged family members
- **Blended Family Issues**: Complex family structures

### Part 17: Tax Planning
- **Tax Concerns**: Any specific tax planning goals
- **Income Splitting**: Desired distribution to minimize taxes
- **Charitable Giving**: Tax-deductible charitable donations
- **Capital Gains**: Strategies for capital gains tax
- **Estate Equalization**: Equalizing gifts to avoid family conflict

### Part 18: Digital Assets & Online Accounts
- **Email Accounts**: Providers and access information
- **Social Media**: Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram accounts
- **Online Banking**: Bank websites and account access
- **Cryptocurrency**: Bitcoin, Ethereum, other digital assets
- **Online Storage**: Dropbox, Google Drive, iCloud accounts
- **Websites/Blogs**: Any online businesses or content
- **Online Passwords**: Secure access information

### Part 19: Funeral & Burial Wishes
- **Burial vs. Cremation**: Preference
- **Funeral Location**: Preferred funeral home or location
- **Religious Preferences**: Any religious ceremonies
- **Memorial Service**: Desired type of memorial
- **Burial Location**: Preferred cemetery
- **Cost Allocation**: Who should pay for funeral

### Part 20: Special Wishes & Instructions
- **Personal Messages**: Letters or messages for beneficiaries
- **Charitable Donations**: Preferred charities
- **Artwork/Heirlooms**: Specific instructions for valuable items
- **Pet Care**: Arrangements for pets
- **Business Succession**: Detailed succession plans
- **Family Harmony**: Instructions to minimize family conflict

## Key Insights from Professional Questionnaires

### Comprehensive Coverage
Professional law firm questionnaires cover 20+ major categories with multiple sub-questions, ensuring thorough estate planning.

### Conditional Logic
Questions build on previous answers:
- "Do you have children?" → If yes, ask for details on each child
- "Do you have business interests?" → If yes, ask about ownership structure, agreements, etc.

### Relationship Mapping
Detailed information on relationships between testator, beneficiaries, and family members to identify potential conflicts.

### Asset Categorization
Assets are organized into distinct categories (financial, real property, personal property, business) with specific questions for each type.

### Legal Compliance
Questions ensure Ontario legal requirements are met:
- Capacity verification
- Proper identification
- Witness information
- Execution requirements

### Risk Identification
Questions designed to identify potential issues:
- Family conflict
- Capacity concerns
- Estrangement
- Special needs beneficiaries
- Complex business structures

## Recommendations for Robust Will Creator

1. **Expand to 40+ Questions**: Cover all 20 categories identified above
2. **Implement Conditional Logic**: Ask follow-up questions based on previous answers
3. **Add Detailed Sub-Questions**: For each major category, ask specific follow-up questions
4. **Include Capacity Assessment**: Verify testator's mental capacity
5. **Identify Risk Factors**: Flag potential issues (estrangement, conflict, special needs)
6. **Provide Context**: Explain why each question matters
7. **Offer Examples**: Provide examples for complex questions
8. **Enable Document Attachment**: Allow upload of existing agreements, court orders
9. **Create Summary Review**: Show all information before document generation
10. **Generate Professional Output**: Create will with proper legal language and formatting
