# Comprehensive 50-Question Ontario POA for Property Questionnaire

## SECTION 1: GRANTOR IDENTIFICATION & CAPACITY (Questions 1-6)

1. **Full Legal Name** - What is your complete legal name?
2. **Date of Birth** - What is your date of birth?
3. **Current Address** - What is your current residential address?
4. **Mental Capacity Confirmation** - Do you confirm that you are of sound mind and understand the nature and consequences of executing a Power of Attorney?
5. **Marital Status** - What is your current marital status?
6. **Spouse Information** - If married/common-law, what is your spouse's full legal name?

## SECTION 2: ATTORNEY SELECTION (Questions 7-15)

7. **Primary Attorney Name** - Who should be your primary attorney for property? Provide full name and address.
8. **Primary Attorney Relationship** - What is your relationship to the primary attorney (family member, friend, professional)?
9. **Primary Attorney Willingness** - Have you discussed this with the primary attorney and confirmed their willingness?
10. **Alternate Attorney** - Who should be your alternate attorney if the primary cannot serve?
11. **Co-Attorney** - Do you want to appoint a co-attorney to act jointly with the primary attorney?
12. **Attorney Powers** - Should the attorney have broad powers or limited powers?
13. **Attorney Compensation** - Should the attorney receive compensation? If yes, how much?
14. **Expense Reimbursement** - Should the attorney be reimbursed for reasonable expenses?
15. **Successor Attorney** - Who should be the successor attorney if both primary and alternate cannot serve?

## SECTION 3: EFFECTIVE DATE & TRIGGERING (Questions 16-20)

16. **Effective Date** - When should this POA become effective (immediately or upon incapacity)?
17. **Springing POA** - If effective upon incapacity, what event triggers effectiveness?
18. **Capacity Determination** - Who should determine if you have lost capacity (doctor, court)?
19. **Revocation Clause** - Can the attorney revoke this POA without your consent?
20. **Duration** - How long should this POA remain in effect (until revoked, until death, specific date)?

## SECTION 4: BANKING & FINANCIAL ACCOUNTS (Questions 21-28)

21. **Primary Bank Account** - List your primary bank account (institution, account type, account number if available).
22. **Additional Bank Accounts** - List any additional bank accounts.
23. **Investment Accounts** - List investment accounts (stocks, bonds, mutual funds).
24. **RRSPs** - Do you have RRSPs? If yes, list institutions and account numbers.
25. **TFSAs** - Do you have TFSAs? If yes, list institutions and account numbers.
26. **GICs & Fixed Income** - Do you have GICs or other fixed income investments?
27. **Safe Deposit Box** - Do you have a safe deposit box? If yes, where and what access should the attorney have?
28. **Financial Account Restrictions** - Are there any restrictions on attorney access to specific accounts?

## SECTION 5: REAL PROPERTY MANAGEMENT (Questions 29-35)

29. **Primary Residence** - Do you own your primary residence? If yes, provide address and ownership details.
30. **Investment Properties** - Do you own investment properties? If yes, list addresses and details.
31. **Vacation Properties** - Do you own vacation properties? If yes, provide details.
32. **Property Sales Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to sell real property?
33. **Property Purchase Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to purchase real property?
34. **Mortgage Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to borrow against real property?
35. **Property Management** - Should the attorney manage rental properties or collect rent?

## SECTION 6: INVESTMENT MANAGEMENT (Questions 36-40)

36. **Investment Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to manage investments?
37. **Investment Types** - Should the attorney be able to invest in stocks, bonds, mutual funds, etc.?
38. **Investment Restrictions** - Are there any investment restrictions or preferences?
39. **Dividend & Interest** - Should the attorney collect dividends and interest?
40. **Investment Liquidation** - Should the attorney have authority to liquidate investments?

## SECTION 7: BUSINESS INTERESTS (Questions 41-44)

41. **Business Ownership** - Do you own or have an interest in any business?
42. **Business Management** - Should the attorney have authority to manage the business?
43. **Business Sale Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to sell the business?
44. **Business Succession** - Are there specific instructions for business management during incapacity?

## SECTION 8: TAX & ACCOUNTING (Questions 45-48)

45. **Tax Return Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to file tax returns?
46. **CRA Dealings** - Should the attorney have authority to deal with Canada Revenue Agency?
47. **Accounting Records** - Should the attorney maintain detailed accounting records?
48. **Accountant Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to hire accountants or tax professionals?

## SECTION 9: DIGITAL & CRYPTOCURRENCY ASSETS (Questions 49-50)

49. **Digital Accounts** - Do you have digital accounts (PayPal, online banking, etc.)? Should the attorney have access?
50. **Cryptocurrency** - Do you own cryptocurrency? Should the attorney have authority to manage it?

## DOCUMENT GENERATION REQUIREMENTS

### POA for Property Opening Clause
"I, [Full Name], of [Address], being of sound mind, do hereby appoint [Attorney Name] of [Address] as my attorney for property."

### Powers Granted Clause
"I hereby grant to my attorney full power and authority to do all things that I could do if I were personally present, including but not limited to: [list specific powers]."

### Specific Powers (if limited POA)
"I grant to my attorney authority over the following matters only: [list specific powers]."

### Limitations Clause
"Notwithstanding the foregoing, my attorney shall not have authority to: [list restrictions]."

### Effective Date Clause
"This Power of Attorney shall be effective [immediately / upon my incapacity as determined by: (specify method)]."

### Compensation Clause
"My attorney shall be entitled to compensation of [amount/percentage] for services rendered."

### Attorney Duties Clause
"My attorney shall: (a) act honestly and in my best interest; (b) maintain detailed records of all transactions; (c) avoid conflicts of interest; (d) not commingle my funds with their own funds."

### Successor Attorney Clause
"If [Primary Attorney] is unable or unwilling to act, I appoint [Alternate Attorney] as my attorney."

### Execution Clause
"IN WITNESS WHEREOF I have hereunto set my hand this [date].

SIGNED, PUBLISHED AND DECLARED by the above-named [Grantor Name] as their Power of Attorney for Property in the presence of us both, present at the same time, who at their request and in their presence and in the presence of each other have hereunto subscribed our names as witnesses."
