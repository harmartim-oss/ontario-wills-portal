# Ontario Will Creation - Legal Requirements & Compliance Guide

## Overview
This document outlines the comprehensive legal requirements for creating a valid Ontario Will under the Succession Law Reform Act (SLRA) and Trustees Act, ensuring the application guides users through all necessary components.

---

## PART 1: TESTATOR QUALIFICATIONS & CAPACITY

### Who Can Make a Will (Section 2, SLRA)
- **Age**: Must be at least 18 years old
- **Exceptions for Minors** (Section 8, SLRA):
  - Married or were married
  - Thinking about marriage and will states this intention
  - Member of Canadian Forces or sailor at sea/on voyage

### Mental Capacity Requirements
- Must understand what property they have
- Must understand what they are doing by making a will
- Must understand the natural claims on their estate (family members)
- Must not be suffering from delusions affecting the will

## PART 2: FORMAL REQUIREMENTS FOR VALID WILL EXECUTION

### Section 3: Will Must Be in Writing
- Will must be written (not oral)
- Can be typed or handwritten (formal will)
- Can be entirely handwritten (holograph will)

### Section 4: Execution Requirements (Formal Will)

**Standard Formal Will Requires:**

1. **Signature at End of Will**
   - Testator signs at the end, OR
   - Someone else signs in testator's presence and by direction

2. **Witness Requirements**
   - Minimum 2 attesting witnesses present at same time
   - Witnesses must be present when testator signs or acknowledges signature
   - Witnesses must see testator sign or acknowledge signature
   - Witnesses must subscribe (sign) the will in testator's presence

3. **Witness Restrictions**
   - Witnesses cannot be beneficiaries under the will (Section 12)
   - Witnesses cannot be spouses of beneficiaries
   - Witnesses cannot be creditors of the estate (Section 13)
   - Executors can be witnesses (Section 14)

4. **Audio-Visual Communication Technology** (2021 Amendment)
   - Presence requirement can be satisfied via video conference
   - At least one witness must be a lawyer (Law Society licensee)
   - Signing and subscribing must be contemporaneous
   - Counterpart signing allowed (identical copies signed separately)

### Section 5: Military Wills
- Members of Canadian Forces on active service can make simplified wills
- Sailors at sea can make simplified wills
- Reduced formality requirements apply

### Section 6: Holograph Wills
- Entirely handwritten by testator
- Signed by testator
- No witnesses required
- Must be dated
- Must clearly state testator's intent
- More vulnerable to challenge than formal wills

### Section 7: Position of Signature
- Signature should be at the end of the will
- Signature in other positions may still be valid if intent is clear

## PART 3: WILL CONTENT REQUIREMENTS

### Essential Components of a Valid Will

1. **Testator Information**
   - Full legal name
   - Current address
   - Date of birth (helpful for identification)
   - Statement of testator's sound mind

2. **Declaration of Intent**
   - Clear statement that this is the testator's last will and testament
   - Revocation of all previous wills and codicils

3. **Family Information**
   - Names and relationships of spouse (if any)
   - Names and ages of all children
   - Statement regarding children not mentioned (if applicable)
   - Names of grandchildren if relevant to bequests

4. **Executor/Estate Trustee Appointment** (Section 29, SLRA)
   - Name of primary executor/estate trustee
   - Name of alternate executor/estate trustee
   - Powers granted to executor
   - Whether executor can be bonded or not
   - Compensation for executor (if any)

5. **Asset Inventory & Distribution**
   - Specific bequests (personal items, jewelry, vehicles)
   - Monetary bequests
   - Real property distribution
   - Residuary estate (remaining property)
   - Conditions or contingencies on bequests

6. **Guardianship Provisions** (if applicable)
   - Guardian for minor children
   - Alternate guardian
   - Guardian for property management

7. **Trustee Appointments** (if trusts are created)
   - Trustee for any trusts created in the will
   - Powers and duties of trustee
   - Distribution instructions for trust beneficiaries

8. **Witness Attestation Clause**
   - Formal statement by witnesses
   - Confirmation of testator's capacity
   - Confirmation of proper execution

## PART 4: TRUSTEES ACT COMPLIANCE (RSO 1990, c. T.23)

### Trustee Duties & Responsibilities

**Fiduciary Duties (Core Obligations):**

1. **Duty of Care**
   - Act with prudence and diligence
   - Exercise degree of care a prudent person would use
   - Manage trust property as if it were their own

2. **Duty of Loyalty**
   - Act in best interests of beneficiaries
   - Avoid conflicts of interest
   - Cannot profit from trust position (except as agreed)

3. **Duty to Account**
   - Keep detailed records of all transactions
   - Provide accounting to beneficiaries upon request
   - Maintain transparency

4. **Duty of Impartiality**
   - Treat all beneficiaries fairly ("even hand" principle)
   - Balance interests of income and capital beneficiaries
   - Cannot favor one beneficiary over another without justification

5. **Duty to Invest Prudently**
   - Invest trust funds in prudent manner
   - Diversify investments appropriately
   - Consider risk and return balance

### Trustee Powers (Section 17+, Trustees Act)

1. **Power of Sale**
   - Sell trust property
   - Lease trust property
   - Mortgage trust property

2. **Power to Invest**
   - Invest in authorized investments
   - Reinvest proceeds from sales
   - Diversify holdings

3. **Power to Delegate**
   - Appoint solicitors, accountants, agents
   - Delegate specific tasks (not fiduciary decisions)
   - Remain liable for agent's conduct

4. **Power to Distribute**
   - Distribute income and capital as directed
   - Exercise discretion in discretionary trusts
   - Make distributions in kind or cash

## PART 5: REVOCATION & AMENDMENTS

### Section 15: Revocation Generally
- By making a new will
- By destroying the will with intent to revoke
- By marriage (unless will states otherwise)
- By divorce (revokes provisions for former spouse)

### Section 18: Alterations
- Changes to will must be initialed by testator and witnesses
- Unsigned alterations may not be valid
- Codicils used to formally amend wills

### Section 21: Revival
- Revoked will can be revived
- Must be re-executed with proper formalities
- Or by codicil referring to revoked will

---

## PART 6: SPECIAL PROVISIONS & CONSIDERATIONS

### Spousal Rights (Section 43.1, SLRA)
- Separated spouses have limited intestacy rights
- Will should address spousal provisions clearly
- Consider Family Law Act implications

### Children's Rights (Section 57+, SLRA)
- Children have right to support from estate
- Court can order support for dependent children
- Will should address minor children's provisions

### Dependents' Support (Part V, SLRA)
- Court can order support for dependents not adequately provided for
- Includes spouses, children, parents, grandchildren
- Will should justify any exclusions

### Henson Trusts (Special Needs Planning)
- Absolute discretionary trusts for disabled beneficiaries
- Protects government benefits (ODSP, CPP-D)
- Requires specific trust language

## PART 9: DOCUMENT GENERATION & FORMATTING

### PDF Document Structure
1. Cover page with testator name and date
2. Full will text with proper legal formatting
3. Signature pages (testator + 2 witnesses)
4. Attestation clause
5. Optional: Explanatory notes for executor

### Legal Formatting Standards
- 1-inch margins
- 12-point font (Times New Roman or similar)
- Double spacing for body text
- Numbered paragraphs
- Proper legal citations
- Page numbers

### Compliance Validation
- Check all required elements present
- Verify witness requirements met
- Confirm proper signatures
- Validate against SLRA sections 3-7
- Flag any potential challenges
