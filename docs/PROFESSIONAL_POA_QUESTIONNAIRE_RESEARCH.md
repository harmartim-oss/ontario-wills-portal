# Professional POA Questionnaire Research - Ontario Law Firms

## Overview
This document compiles research findings from professional Ontario law firm POA intake forms and questionnaires to inform the development of comprehensive, robust POA creators for Property and Personal Care.

## Key Sources Reviewed
1. **MAP Law - Will Intake Form for Wills and POAs** (2015)
2. **Advent Legal - Power of Attorney Intake Form** (Scarborough)
3. **Ontario Publications - Powers of Attorney (PDF version)** - Official Government Forms
4. **WOW Law - Will Intake Questionnaire** (includes POA sections)

## POA for Property - Comprehensive Questionnaire Structure

### Section 1: Grantor Information & Identification
- **Full Legal Name**: As it appears on government-issued ID
- **Alternative Names**: Maiden names, aliases, previous names
- **Date of Birth**: For capacity verification
- **Place of Birth**: For identification purposes
- **Citizenship/SIN**: For tax and identification
- **Current Address**: Residence and contact information
- **Phone & Email**: Contact details
- **Occupation & Employer**: For financial context
- **Marital Status**: Single, married, common law, divorced, widowed
- **Spouse Information**: Name and relationship

### Section 2: Capacity Assessment & Medical History
- **Capacity Verification**: Any capacity-related diagnosis or concerns
- **Mental Health History**: Relevant mental health conditions
- **Cognitive Concerns**: Memory issues, confusion, dementia risk
- **Substance Abuse History**: Any history of substance abuse
- **Current Medications**: Relevant medications affecting capacity
- **Physician Contact**: Doctor's name and contact for verification

### Section 3: Attorney Selection - Primary Attorney
- **Attorney's Full Legal Name**: As on government ID
- **Relationship to Grantor**: Family member, friend, professional, corporate trustee
- **Attorney's Address**: Current residence
- **Attorney's Age**: Confirmation of adult status
- **Attorney's Occupation**: Professional background
- **Attorney's Contact Information**: Phone and email
- **Attorney's Willingness to Serve**: Confirmation of acceptance
- **Attorney's Experience**: Prior experience as POA attorney
- **Attorney's Financial Stability**: General assessment of financial responsibility
- **Conflict of Interest Check**: Any conflicts with other beneficiaries or interests

### Section 4: Alternate Attorney - Primary Alternate

## Key Insights from Professional POA Questionnaires

### Comprehensive Coverage
Professional law firm POA questionnaires cover 20+ major categories with multiple sub-questions, ensuring thorough estate planning.

### Conditional Logic
Questions build on previous answers to gather relevant information based on grantor's circumstances.

### Attorney Vetting
Detailed questions about attorney selection, qualifications, and willingness to serve ensure appropriate appointment.

### Powers Specification
Detailed questions about specific powers (real estate, financial, business, tax, insurance, etc.) allow grantor to customize POA scope.

### Healthcare Preferences
Detailed questions about life-sustaining treatment, end-of-life care, and healthcare decisions ensure grantor's wishes are known.

### Capacity Verification
Questions about capacity, medical history, and physician contact ensure grantor has capacity to create POA.

### Risk Identification
Questions designed to identify potential issues (family conflict, capacity concerns, inappropriate attorney selection).

## Recommendations for Robust POA Creators

1. **Expand to 35+ Questions Each**: Cover all 20 categories identified above for both POA Property and POA Personal Care
2. **Implement Conditional Logic**: Ask follow-up questions based on previous answers
3. **Add Detailed Sub-Questions**: For each major category, ask specific follow-up questions
4. **Include Capacity Assessment**: Verify grantor's mental capacity
5. **Identify Risk Factors**: Flag potential issues (inappropriate attorney, family conflict, capacity concerns)
6. **Provide Context**: Explain why each question matters
7. **Offer Examples**: Provide examples for complex questions
8. **Enable Document Attachment**: Allow upload of existing agreements, medical directives
9. **Create Summary Review**: Show all information before document generation
10. **Generate Professional Output**: Create POA with proper legal language and formatting
