# Comprehensive 50-Question Ontario POA for Personal Care Questionnaire

## SECTION 1: GRANTOR IDENTIFICATION & CAPACITY (Questions 1-6)

1. **Full Legal Name** - What is your complete legal name?
2. **Date of Birth** - What is your date of birth?
3. **Current Address** - What is your current residential address?
4. **Mental Capacity Confirmation** - Do you confirm that you are of sound mind and understand the nature and consequences of executing a Power of Attorney for Personal Care?
5. **Marital Status** - What is your current marital status?
6. **Healthcare Provider** - Who is your primary healthcare provider?

## SECTION 2: ATTORNEY SELECTION (Questions 7-12)

7. **Primary Attorney Name** - Who should be your primary attorney for personal care? Provide full name and address.
8. **Primary Attorney Relationship** - What is your relationship to the primary attorney?
9. **Primary Attorney Willingness** - Have you discussed this with the primary attorney and confirmed their willingness?
10. **Alternate Attorney** - Who should be your alternate attorney if the primary cannot serve?
11. **Co-Attorney** - Do you want to appoint a co-attorney to act jointly with the primary attorney?
12. **Successor Attorney** - Who should be the successor attorney if both primary and alternate cannot serve?

## SECTION 3: HEALTHCARE DECISION AUTHORITY (Questions 13-18)

13. **General Healthcare Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to make general healthcare decisions?
14. **Specific Healthcare Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to make specific healthcare decisions (list types)?
15. **Psychiatric Treatment Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to make psychiatric treatment decisions?
16. **Refusal of Treatment Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to refuse medical treatment?
17. **Life-Sustaining Treatment Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to make life-sustaining treatment decisions?
18. **Organ Donation Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to make organ/tissue donation decisions?

## SECTION 4: LIFE-SUSTAINING TREATMENT PREFERENCES (Questions 19-25)

19. **CPR Preference** - If your heart stops, do you want CPR (cardiopulmonary resuscitation)?
20. **Mechanical Ventilation Preference** - If you cannot breathe on your own, do you want mechanical ventilation?
21. **Artificial Nutrition Preference** - If you cannot eat or drink, do you want artificial nutrition and hydration?
22. **Dialysis Preference** - If your kidneys fail, do you want dialysis?
23. **Blood Transfusion Preference** - Do you want blood transfusions if needed?
24. **Antibiotics Preference** - Do you want antibiotics for infections?
25. **Resuscitation Preference** - Do you have a DNR (Do Not Resuscitate) preference?

## SECTION 5: MENTAL HEALTH TREATMENT (Questions 26-30)

26. **Psychiatric Medication Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to consent to psychiatric medications?
27. **Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT)** - Should the attorney have authority to consent to ECT?
28. **Psychiatric Hospitalization Authority** - Should the attorney have authority to consent to psychiatric hospitalization?
29. **Psychotherapy Preference** - Do you have preferences regarding psychotherapy or counseling?
30. **Mental Health Conditions** - Do you have any mental health conditions the attorney should know about?

## SECTION 6: PALLIATIVE & END-OF-LIFE CARE (Questions 31-37)

31. **Palliative Care Preference** - Do you want palliative (comfort) care if you have a terminal illness?
32. **Pain Management Preference** - How important is pain management to you?
33. **Hospice Preference** - Would you prefer hospice care at end of life?
34. **Place of Death Preference** - Where would you prefer to die (home, hospital, facility)?
35. **Funeral Preferences** - Do you have funeral preferences (traditional, cremation, etc.)?
36. **Burial Location** - Where should you be buried or where should your ashes be scattered?
37. **Memorial Service Preferences** - Do you want a memorial service? If yes, what type?

## SECTION 7: MEDICAL CONDITIONS & PREFERENCES (Questions 38-43)

38. **Chronic Conditions** - What chronic medical conditions do you have?
39. **Medication Allergies** - Do you have any medication allergies?
40. **Medical Sensitivities** - Are there any medications or treatments you should avoid?
41. **Preferred Healthcare Providers** - Do you have preferred doctors or hospitals?
42. **Religious/Cultural Healthcare Preferences** - Do you have religious or cultural healthcare preferences?
43. **Previous Healthcare Experiences** - Are there previous healthcare experiences that should inform decisions?

## SECTION 8: PERSONAL CARE PREFERENCES (Questions 44-48)

44. **Living Arrangement Preferences** - Where would you prefer to live if you need care (home, facility, etc.)?
45. **Daily Routine Preferences** - What daily routines are important to you?
46. **Social Activity Preferences** - What social activities are important to you?
47. **Spiritual/Religious Care** - What spiritual or religious care is important to you?
48. **Hygiene & Grooming Preferences** - Do you have specific hygiene or grooming preferences?

## SECTION 9: CORE VALUES & QUALITY OF LIFE (Questions 49-50)

49. **Core Values** - What are your core values that should guide healthcare decisions?
50. **Quality of Life Threshold** - What conditions would make life not worth living to you?

## CONDITIONAL FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS (Based on Answers)

### If grantor has terminal illness or serious health condition:
- Q51: Should the attorney prioritize comfort over life extension?
- Q52: Should the attorney consider your wishes if they conflict with medical advice?
- Q53: Should the attorney consult with family members on major decisions?

### If grantor has religious/cultural beliefs:
- Q54: Should the attorney ensure religious practices are followed?
- Q55: Should the attorney consult with religious leaders?
- Q56: Are there specific religious restrictions on medical treatment?

### If grantor has mental health concerns:
- Q57: Should the attorney have authority to involuntarily hospitalize you?
- Q58: Should the attorney restrict certain medications?
- Q59: Should the attorney consult with mental health professionals?

### If grantor has family dynamics concerns:
- Q60: Should the attorney consult with family members on decisions?
- Q61: Should the attorney be restricted from certain family members' influence?
- Q62: Should decisions be made by the attorney alone or with consultation?

### If grantor has specific medical wishes:
- Q63: Should the attorney honor your wishes even if they conflict with medical advice?
- Q64: Should the attorney seek second opinions on major decisions?
- Q65: Should the attorney document all major decisions made?

## DOCUMENT GENERATION REQUIREMENTS

### POA for Personal Care Opening Clause
"I, [Full Name], of [Address], being of sound mind, do hereby appoint [Attorney Name] of [Address] as my attorney for personal care."

### Healthcare Authority Clause
"I hereby grant to my attorney full power and authority to make healthcare decisions on my behalf, including but not limited to: [list specific authorities]."

### Life-Sustaining Treatment Clause
"Regarding life-sustaining treatment, I direct my attorney as follows: [list specific preferences for CPR, ventilation, nutrition, etc.]."

### Mental Health Treatment Clause
"Regarding mental health treatment, I direct my attorney as follows: [list specific preferences for psychiatric care, medications, hospitalization, etc.]."

### Palliative Care Clause
"If I have a terminal illness or am in a persistent vegetative state, I direct my attorney to prioritize [comfort care / life extension / my wishes] and to: [list specific instructions]."

### Personal Care Preferences Clause
"Regarding my personal care, I prefer: [list preferences for living arrangements, daily routines, social activities, spiritual care, etc.]."

### Core Values Clause
"My core values that should guide healthcare decisions are: [list core values]. I consider my quality of life unacceptable if: [list unacceptable conditions]."

### Attorney Duties Clause
"My attorney shall: (a) act honestly and in my best interest; (b) follow my wishes and values; (c) consult with healthcare providers; (d) consider my quality of life; (e) document major decisions."

### Successor Attorney Clause
"If [Primary Attorney] is unable or unwilling to act, I appoint [Alternate Attorney] as my attorney for personal care."

### Execution Clause
"IN WITNESS WHEREOF I have hereunto set my hand this [date].

SIGNED, PUBLISHED AND DECLARED by the above-named [Grantor Name] as their Power of Attorney for Personal Care in the presence of us both, present at the same time, who at their request and in their presence and in the presence of each other have hereunto subscribed our names as witnesses."
