# Enhanced Will and POA Questionnaire Design

## Overview
This document outlines the comprehensive questionnaire structure for creating professional-grade Ontario Wills and Powers of Attorney with 20+ detailed questions each.

---

## PART 1: ENHANCED WILL QUESTIONNAIRE (20+ Questions)

### Section 1: Testator Information & Capacity (Questions 1-4)

**Q1: Personal Identification**
- Full legal name (as it appears on government ID)
- Preferred name (if different)
- Date of birth
- Current residential address
- Province of residence
- Citizenship status

**Q2: Mental Capacity Affirmation**
- Confirm you are of sound mind and memory
- Confirm you understand the nature and extent of your property
- Confirm you understand the natural claims on your estate
- Confirm you are not under undue influence

**Q3: Previous Wills**
- Have you made a will before? (Yes/No)
- If yes, when was the last will made?
- Do you want to revoke all previous wills? (Yes/No)
- Are there any provisions from previous wills you want to retain?

**Q4: Marital & Family Status**
- Current marital status (Single/Married/Common-law/Divorced/Widowed)
- If married/common-law: Spouse name, date of marriage
- If divorced: Date of divorce, any support obligations
- If widowed: Date of spouse's death

---

### Section 2: Family Structure & Relationships (Questions 5-8)

**Q5: Children & Grandchildren**
- Do you have any children? (Yes/No)
- If yes: List each child with name, date of birth, current age
- Do any of your children have children (your grandchildren)? (Yes/No)
- If yes: List grandchildren with names, ages, and which child is their parent

**Q6: Dependents & Special Circumstances**
- Do you have any dependent children under 18? (Yes/No)
- Do you have any adult children with disabilities? (Yes/No)
- Do you have any estranged or estranged family members you want to specifically exclude?
- Do you have any children from previous relationships?

**Q7: Blended Family Considerations**
- Is this a blended family situation? (Yes/No)
- If yes: How many children from current relationship vs. previous relationships?
- Are there any concerns about fairness between biological and step-children?
- Do you want to make different provisions for different children?

**Q8: Parental Status**
- Are your parents still living? (Yes/No)
- If yes: Do you want to make any provisions for them?
- Are there any elderly relatives you support financially?
- Do you want to make any charitable bequests?

---

### Section 3: Asset Inventory & Valuation (Questions 9-12)

**Q9: Real Property**
- Do you own any real estate? (Yes/No)
- If yes, for each property:
  - Address and legal description
  - Type (primary residence, rental, investment, cottage, etc.)
  - Estimated current value
  - Mortgage/lien status and amount owing
  - How is it titled (sole, joint, tenancy in common)?
  - Any special considerations (heritage property, environmental issues)?

**Q10: Financial Assets**
- Do you have bank accounts? (Yes/No)
  - List institutions, account types, estimated balances
- Do you have investments (stocks, bonds, mutual funds)? (Yes/No)
  - Estimated total value
  - Are they registered (RRSP, TFSA, etc.) or non-registered?
- Do you have life insurance policies? (Yes/No)
  - Who is the beneficiary currently named?
  - Do you want to change the beneficiary?
- Do you have pension or retirement accounts? (Yes/No)
  - Type and estimated value
  - Any designated beneficiaries?

**Q11: Business & Professional Interests**
- Do you own a business or professional practice? (Yes/No)
- If yes:
  - Business name and type
  - Your ownership percentage
  - Estimated value
  - Are there buy-sell agreements in place?
  - Do you want the business to continue or be sold?
  - Who would you want to run it?

**Q12: Digital Assets & Cryptocurrency**
- Do you own cryptocurrency? (Yes/No)
  - Types and estimated value
  - Where are private keys stored?
- Do you have valuable digital assets (domain names, online businesses, NFTs)? (Yes/No)
- Do you have online accounts with significant value (PayPal, online stores)?
- Do you have valuable intellectual property (patents, trademarks, copyrights)?

---

### Section 4: Beneficiary Planning (Questions 13-15)

**Q13: Primary Beneficiaries & Distribution**
- For each beneficiary, specify:
  - Name and relationship
  - Specific bequest or percentage of estate
  - Any conditions on the bequest
  - Should they receive this during your lifetime or only after death?

**Q14: Contingent & Alternate Beneficiaries**
- What should happen if a primary beneficiary dies before you?
- Should their share go to their children (per stirpes)?
- Or should it be redistributed to other beneficiaries?
- Do you want to name specific alternate beneficiaries?

**Q15: Special Beneficiary Situations**
- Do you want to leave money to minors? (Yes/No)
  - Should it be held in trust until they reach a certain age?
  - Who should manage the trust?
- Do you want to leave money to someone with a disability? (Yes/No)
  - Should it be in a Henson Trust to protect government benefits?
- Do you want to make charitable donations? (Yes/No)
  - Which charities and how much?

---

### Section 5: Executor & Trustee Appointments (Questions 16-17)

**Q16: Executor Selection & Powers**
- Who do you want as your primary executor?
  - Name, relationship, address
  - Are they willing and able?
- Who should be the alternate executor?
- What powers should the executor have?
  - Power to sell assets?
  - Power to invest and reinvest?
  - Power to pay debts and taxes?
  - Power to distribute to beneficiaries?
- Should the executor be compensated?
  - Flat fee, percentage of estate, or as allowed by law?

**Q17: Trustee Appointments (if creating trusts)**
- Who should be the trustee for any trusts?
- Should the executor also be the trustee?
- What powers should the trustee have?
- Should the trustee have discretion in distributions?
- How should income and capital be distributed?

---

### Section 6: Guardian & Custodian Appointments (Questions 18-19)

**Q18: Guardian for Minor Children**
- Do you have minor children? (Yes/No)
- If yes:
  - Who should be the guardian of the person?
  - Who should be the guardian of the property/finances?
  - Can these be the same person or different?
  - Who should be the alternate guardian?
  - Have you discussed this with the proposed guardians?

**Q19: Custodian for Minors' Inheritances**
- If minor children will inherit money:
  - Who should manage their inheritance?
  - Should it be held in trust until they reach a certain age?
  - What age should they receive full control (18, 21, 25)?
  - Should distributions be made incrementally?

---

### Section 7: Special Provisions & Conditions (Questions 20-22)

**Q20: Specific Bequests & Conditions**
- Are there specific items you want specific people to have?
  - Jewelry, artwork, vehicles, collections, etc.
  - Who gets each item?
- Are there any conditions on bequests?
  - "Only if they reach age 25"
  - "Only if they maintain sobriety"
  - "Only if they complete university"

**Q21: Wishes & Instructions**
- Do you have wishes regarding:
  - Funeral arrangements (burial, cremation, etc.)?
  - Who should organize your funeral?
  - Any specific requests or preferences?
- Do you want to leave letters or messages for beneficiaries?
- Are there any pets that need to be cared for?
  - Who should care for them?
  - Should money be left for their care?

**Q22: Debt & Tax Considerations**
- Do you have significant debts (mortgages, loans, credit cards)?
  - Should these be paid from the estate?
  - Or should the beneficiary assume them?
- Do you have tax concerns?
  - Capital gains on investments?
  - Income from rental properties?
  - Business succession tax issues?
- Should the executor have power to make tax elections?

---

### Section 8: Review & Compliance (Questions 23-25)

**Q23: Witness & Execution Details**
- Who will be your witnesses?
  - Name, address, relationship
  - Confirm they are not beneficiaries
  - Confirm they are not spouses of beneficiaries
- When and where will you sign the will?
- Will you use video conferencing for execution?

**Q24: Document Storage & Access**
- Where will you store the original will?
  - Home safe, lawyer's office, bank safety deposit box?
- Who should know where it is stored?
- Should the executor have access to your important documents?
- Do you have a list of your assets and account information?

**Q25: Professional Review**
- Do you want a lawyer to review this will?
- Are there complex family or financial situations?
- Do you have concerns about will validity or challenges?

## PART 2: ENHANCED POA FOR PROPERTY QUESTIONNAIRE (20+ Questions)

### Section 1: Grantor Information (Questions 1-2)

**Q1: Personal Identification**
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Current residential address
- Confirmation of sound mind and understanding

**Q2: Purpose & Scope**
- Do you want a general POA or limited POA?
- What specific powers do you want to delegate?
  - Real estate transactions?
  - Financial account management?
  - Investment decisions?
  - Business management?

---

### Section 2: Attorney Selection (Questions 3-5)

**Q3: Primary Attorney**
- Who do you want as your primary attorney?
  - Name, relationship, address
  - Are they willing and able?
  - Do they have financial management experience?

**Q4: Successor Attorneys**
- Who should act if the primary attorney is unable?
- Should multiple attorneys act jointly or separately?
- If jointly, can they act independently or only together?

**Q5: Attorney Compensation**
- Should the attorney be compensated?
- If yes, how much or what percentage?
- Should they be reimbursed for expenses?

---

### Section 3: Powers & Limitations (Questions 6-10)

**Q6: Real Estate Powers**
- Should the attorney have power to:
  - Buy/sell real property?
  - Mortgage or refinance property?
  - Lease property?
  - Manage rental properties?
  - Collect rents?

**Q7: Financial Account Powers**
- Should the attorney have power to:
  - Deposit/withdraw funds?
  - Pay bills and debts?
  - Manage bank accounts?
  - Access safety deposit boxes?
  - Manage investment accounts?

**Q8: Investment Powers**
- Should the attorney have power to:
  - Buy/sell investments?
  - Manage investment portfolios?
  - Make investment decisions?
  - Delegate to investment advisors?

**Q9: Business Powers**
- Should the attorney have power to:
  - Manage your business?
  - Make business decisions?
  - Hire/fire employees?
  - Enter into contracts?
  - Sell the business?

**Q10: Tax & Accounting Powers**
- Should the attorney have power to:
  - File tax returns?
  - Make tax elections?
  - Deal with CRA?
  - Manage accounting matters?

---

### Section 4: Limitations & Restrictions (Questions 11-12)

**Q11: Specific Limitations**
- Are there any powers you want to exclude?
- Are there any transactions you want to prohibit?
- Should the attorney be limited in how much they can spend?
- Should they need your approval for major transactions?

**Q12: Accountability & Reporting**
- Should the attorney provide regular accountings?
- How often (monthly, quarterly, annually)?
- Should they keep detailed records?
- Should they report to anyone else?

---

### Section 5: Effective Date & Duration (Questions 13-14)

**Q13: When Should It Take Effect?**
- Should it be effective immediately?
- Or only if you become incapacitated?
- How will incapacity be determined?
  - Doctor's certificate?
  - Two doctors?
  - Court order?

**Q14: Duration**
- Should it last indefinitely?
- Or only for a specific period?
- Should it survive your incapacity?
- Should it end on a specific date?

---

### Section 6: Special Circumstances (Questions 15-17)

**Q15: Digital Assets & Accounts**
- Should the attorney have access to:
  - Online banking?
  - Email accounts?
  - Social media accounts?
  - Cryptocurrency wallets?
  - Online businesses?

**Q16: Insurance & Benefits**
- Should the attorney have power to:
  - Manage insurance policies?
  - Make insurance claims?
  - Manage pension accounts?
  - Claim government benefits?

**Q17: Family & Personal Matters**
- Should the attorney have power to:
  - Make gifts to family members?
  - Support dependents?
  - Make charitable donations?
  - Any restrictions on these powers?

---

### Section 7: Successor & Contingency (Questions 18-19)

**Q18: What If Attorney Dies or Becomes Incapacitated?**
- Who should replace them?
- Should multiple successors be named?
- Should they act jointly or separately?

**Q19: Revocation & Termination**
- Can you revoke this POA at any time?
- Should it automatically terminate on a certain date?
- Should it terminate if you recover from incapacity?

---

### Section 8: Review & Execution (Questions 20-22)

**Q20: Witness & Notarization**
- Will this be witnessed?
- Should it be notarized?
- Who will be the witnesses/notary?

**Q21: Document Storage**
- Where will the original be stored?
- Who should have copies?
- Should it be registered anywhere?

**Q22: Communication**
- Have you discussed this with your attorney?
- Does your attorney understand their responsibilities?
- Should they receive a copy before execution?

---

## PART 3: ENHANCED POA FOR PERSONAL CARE QUESTIONNAIRE (15+ Questions)

### Section 1: Grantor Information (Questions 1-2)

**Q1: Personal Identification**
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Current residential address
- Confirmation of sound mind and understanding

**Q2: Purpose**
- Why are you creating this POA?
- Do you have specific health concerns?
- Do you want to plan for future incapacity?

---

### Section 2: Attorney Selection (Questions 3-4)

**Q3: Primary Attorney**
- Who do you want to make personal care decisions?
  - Name, relationship, address
  - Are they willing and able?
  - Do they understand your values and wishes?

**Q4: Successor Attorneys**
- Who should act if the primary attorney is unable?
- Should multiple attorneys act jointly or separately?

---

### Section 3: Health Care Decisions (Questions 5-9)

**Q5: Medical Treatment Preferences**
- Do you want life-sustaining treatment?
  - Resuscitation (CPR)?
  - Mechanical ventilation?
  - Artificial nutrition/hydration?
- Under what circumstances would you want to refuse treatment?

**Q6: Organ Donation**
- Do you want to be an organ donor?
- Are there any restrictions?
- Should your attorney make the decision?

**Q7: Mental Health Treatment**
- Should your attorney have power to consent to:
  - Psychiatric treatment?
  - Medication for mental health?
  - Hospitalization for mental health?

**Q8: Palliative & End-of-Life Care**
- Do you want palliative care?
- Do you want pain management prioritized?
- Do you have wishes about dying at home vs. hospital?

**Q9: Specific Medical Conditions**
- Do you have specific instructions for:
  - Dementia or Alzheimer's?
  - Cancer treatment?
  - Stroke or brain injury?
  - Terminal illness?

---

### Section 4: Personal & Social Decisions (Questions 10-12)

**Q10: Living Arrangements**
- Should your attorney decide where you live?
  - At home with support?
  - In assisted living?
  - In a long-term care facility?
- Any preferences?

**Q11: Social & Recreational Activities**
- Should your attorney encourage social activities?
- Should they make decisions about:
  - Religious or spiritual activities?
  - Visiting family and friends?
  - Hobbies and interests?

**Q12: Personal Grooming & Hygiene**
- Should your attorney make decisions about:
  - Personal hygiene and grooming?
  - Clothing choices?
  - Hair and appearance?

---

### Section 5: Values & Wishes (Questions 13-15)

**Q13: Your Values**
- What are your core values?
- What matters most to you?
- How should these guide decisions?

**Q14: Religious & Spiritual Wishes**
- Do you have religious beliefs?
- Should your attorney respect these?
- Any specific religious practices or restrictions?

**Q15: Quality of Life**
- What would make life worth living for you?
- What would you consider unacceptable?
- How should your attorney balance treatment vs. quality of life?

## Implementation Strategy

### UI/UX Approach
1. **Progressive Disclosure** - Show questions one or two at a time
2. **Conditional Logic** - Skip irrelevant questions based on answers
3. **Helpful Context** - Provide explanations and examples for each question
4. **Progress Tracking** - Show completion percentage and estimated time remaining
5. **Save & Resume** - Allow users to save progress and return later

### Document Generation
1. **Clause Selection** - Use answers to select appropriate clauses
2. **Conditional Clauses** - Include/exclude clauses based on answers
3. **Professional Language** - Generate sophisticated, legally-precise language
4. **Formatting** - Create professionally formatted PDF documents
5. **Compliance Checking** - Validate against Ontario legal requirements

### Quality Assurance
1. **Validation** - Ensure all required information is collected
2. **Consistency Checking** - Verify answers are logically consistent
3. **Compliance Verification** - Check against SLRA, POA Act requirements
4. **Legal Review** - Recommend professional review for complex situations
