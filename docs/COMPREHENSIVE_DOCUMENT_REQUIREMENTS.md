# Comprehensive Ontario Will & POA Document Requirements

## PART 1: ONTARIO WILL REQUIREMENTS & BEST PRACTICES

### Legal Framework
- **Succession Law Reform Act (SLRA)** - Governs formal will requirements in Ontario
- **Formal Requirements (Section 4, SLRA)**:
  - Must be in writing
  - Must be signed by testator
  - Signature must be made with testamentary intent
  - Signature must be made in presence of two witnesses
  - Each witness must acknowledge testator's signature
  - Witnesses must sign in presence of testator and each other
  - Witnesses cannot be beneficiaries or spouses of beneficiaries

### Essential Information for Will Creation

#### 1. TESTATOR INFORMATION (Personal Details)
- Full legal name (including any aliases/maiden names)
- Date of birth
- Current address (residence in Ontario or elsewhere)
- Marital status
- Current spouse name (if applicable)
- Previous marriages (if applicable)
- Children (names, dates of birth, whether adopted)
- Grandchildren (names, dates of birth)
- Dependents (financial support relationships)
- Testamentary capacity confirmation (of sound mind)
- Any mental health considerations

#### 2. FAMILY STRUCTURE & RELATIONSHIPS
- Spouse/partner information
- All children (biological, adopted, step-children)
- Grandchildren details
- Estranged family members (to be excluded)
- Dependent adults (financial support)
- Guardianship arrangements for minor children
  - Primary guardian name and address
  - Alternate guardian name and address
  - Guardian for property (may differ from personal guardian)

#### 3. ASSET INVENTORY & VALUATION
**Real Property:**
- Primary residence (address, estimated value, ownership type)
- Investment properties (addresses, estimated values)
- Vacation properties
- Land holdings
- Mortgages/liens on properties

**Financial Assets:**
- Bank accounts (institution, account type, approximate balance)
- Investment accounts (stocks, bonds, mutual funds)
- RRSPs (registered retirement savings plans)
- TFSAs (tax-free savings accounts)
- GICs and other fixed income
- Pensions (employer, government)
- Life insurance policies (face value, beneficiary designations)

**Business Interests:**
- Business ownership percentage
- Business type and location
- Estimated business value
- Buy-sell agreements in place
- Partnership agreements

**Digital & Cryptocurrency Assets:**
- Cryptocurrency holdings (type, exchange, wallet info)
- Digital accounts (PayPal, online banking, etc.)
- Domain names and websites
- Online business accounts
- Digital subscriptions

**Personal Property:**
- Vehicles (make, model, value)
- Jewelry and collectibles
- Art and antiques
- Household furnishings
- Sentimental items (with specific bequests)
- Tools and equipment

**Debts & Liabilities:**
- Mortgages (lender, balance, property)
- Personal loans
- Credit card debts
- Business debts
- Tax liabilities

#### 4. BENEFICIARY DESIGNATIONS
**Primary Beneficiaries:**
- Full legal name
- Relationship to testator
- Address
- Percentage or specific amount
- Conditions on inheritance

**Alternate Beneficiaries:**
- Contingent beneficiaries if primary predeceases
- Multiple levels of contingency

**Specific Bequests:**
- Specific items to specific people
- Monetary gifts (amounts)
- Percentage of residual estate

**Residual Estate Distribution:**
- Distribution of remaining assets after specific bequests
- Percentage allocation among beneficiaries

**Charitable Bequests:**
- Charitable organizations (registered charity numbers)
- Amounts or percentages

#### 5. EXECUTOR/ESTATE TRUSTEE APPOINTMENT
- Primary executor full name and address
- Executor's relationship to testator
- Executor's willingness and capability
- Alternate executor(s)
- Executor compensation (if any)
- Executor powers and restrictions
- Executor bonding requirements

#### 6. TRUSTEE APPOINTMENTS (if trusts created)
- Primary trustee name and address
- Alternate trustee(s)
- Trustee compensation
- Trustee powers and restrictions
- Trust duration

#### 7. GUARDIAN APPOINTMENTS (for minor children)
- Primary guardian for person (custody)
- Alternate guardian for person
- Guardian for property (financial management)
- Alternate guardian for property
- Guardianship duration
- Guardian compensation (if applicable)

#### 8. TRUST PROVISIONS
**Testamentary Trusts:**
- For minor children
- For beneficiaries with disabilities (Henson trusts)
- For spendthrift beneficiaries
- For beneficiaries with substance abuse issues

**Trust Terms:**
- Age at which beneficiaries receive distributions
- Trustee discretion in distributions
- Income vs. capital distributions
- Trust termination date

**Special Needs Trusts (Henson Trusts):**
- Beneficiary name and disability details
- Government benefits preservation (ODSP/CPP-D)
- Trust corpus (initial funding)
- Trustee instructions on benefit preservation
- Permitted distributions
- Remainder beneficiary (after disabled beneficiary's death)

#### 9. SPECIFIC PROVISIONS & CONDITIONS
- Forgiveness of debts to beneficiaries
- Disinheritance clauses (if applicable)
- Conditions on inheritance (e.g., age, education, behavior)
- Restrictions on asset sales
- Instructions for business succession
- Funeral and burial wishes
- Organ donation preferences

#### 10. TAX PLANNING CONSIDERATIONS
- Probate fee minimization strategies
- Tax-efficient asset distribution
- Spousal rollover provisions
- Capital gains considerations
- Life insurance funding of estate taxes

#### 11. WITNESS INFORMATION
- First witness full name
- First witness address
- First witness relationship to testator (must not be beneficiary)
- Second witness full name
- Second witness address
- Second witness relationship to testator (must not be beneficiary)

#### 12. EXECUTION DETAILS
- Date of will signing
- Location of signing
- Testator signature
- Witness signatures and dates
- Notarization (optional but recommended)

## PART 2: ONTARIO POA FOR PROPERTY REQUIREMENTS

### Legal Framework
- **Substitute Decisions Act, 1992** - Governs POAs in Ontario
- **Continuing Power of Attorney for Property** - Remains valid if grantor loses capacity
- **Non-Continuing Power of Attorney** - Ends if grantor loses capacity

### Essential Information for POA for Property

#### 1. GRANTOR INFORMATION
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Current address
- Confirmation of mental capacity
- Marital status

#### 2. ATTORNEY SELECTION
- Primary attorney full name and address
- Attorney's relationship to grantor
- Attorney's willingness and capability
- Alternate attorney(s)
- Co-attorneys (joint or several authority)

#### 3. SCOPE OF POWERS
**Financial Powers:**
- Banking and financial accounts
- Investment management
- Borrowing on grantor's behalf
- Real property transactions
- Business management
- Tax matters

**Specific Limitations:**
- Cannot make gifts (unless specified)
- Cannot change will
- Cannot delegate powers (unless specified)
- Cannot act after grantor's death

#### 4. EFFECTIVE DATE
- Immediate effectiveness (springing POA triggers on grantor's incapacity)
- Specific triggering event
- Condition for effectiveness

#### 5. COMPENSATION
- Attorney compensation amount
- Reimbursement of expenses
- Accounting requirements

#### 6. ATTORNEY DUTIES & RESTRICTIONS
- Duty to act honestly
- Duty to act in grantor's best interest
- Duty to keep accounts
- Duty to avoid conflicts of interest
- Restrictions on self-dealing

#### 7. REAL PROPERTY DETAILS (if applicable)
- Property addresses
- Property ownership details
- Mortgage information
- Restrictions on property sales

#### 8. FINANCIAL ACCOUNT DETAILS
- Bank accounts (institution, account type)
- Investment accounts
- RRSPs/TFSAs
- Safe deposit boxes
- Access instructions

#### 9. BUSINESS INTERESTS
- Business name and location
- Grantor's ownership percentage
- Business management authority
- Succession planning

#### 10. DIGITAL ASSETS
- Online banking access
- Cryptocurrency holdings
- Digital account passwords/access
- Website/domain management

#### 11. EXECUTION DETAILS
- Date of POA signing
- Grantor signature
- Witness signatures (2 required, not beneficiaries)
- Notarization (recommended)

## PART 3: ONTARIO POA FOR PERSONAL CARE REQUIREMENTS

### Legal Framework
- **Substitute Decisions Act, 1992** - Governs POAs for Personal Care
- **Health Care Consent Act, 1996** - Governs healthcare decision-making
- **Mental Health Act** - Governs psychiatric treatment decisions

### Essential Information for POA for Personal Care

#### 1. GRANTOR INFORMATION
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Current address
- Confirmation of mental capacity

#### 2. ATTORNEY SELECTION
- Primary attorney full name and address
- Attorney's relationship to grantor
- Alternate attorney(s)
- Co-attorneys

#### 3. HEALTHCARE DECISION AUTHORITY
- General healthcare decisions
- Specific healthcare decisions
- Psychiatric treatment authority
- Refusal of treatment authority
- Life-sustaining treatment decisions

#### 4. LIFE-SUSTAINING TREATMENT PREFERENCES
- CPR (cardiopulmonary resuscitation)
- Mechanical ventilation
- Artificial nutrition and hydration
- Dialysis
- Blood transfusions
- Organ/tissue donation

#### 5. MENTAL HEALTH TREATMENT
- Psychiatric medication
- Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT)
- Psychiatric hospitalization
- Psychotherapy preferences

#### 6. PALLIATIVE & END-OF-LIFE CARE
- Pain management preferences
- Comfort care priorities
- Hospice preferences
- Place of death preferences (home, hospital, facility)

#### 7. MEDICAL CONDITIONS & PREFERENCES
- Allergies and medication sensitivities
- Chronic conditions management
- Preferred healthcare providers
- Hospital preferences
- Religious/cultural healthcare preferences

#### 8. PERSONAL CARE PREFERENCES
- Living arrangement preferences
- Daily routine preferences
- Social activity preferences
- Spiritual/religious care

#### 9. CORE VALUES & QUALITY OF LIFE
- What makes life worth living (grantor's perspective)
- Values and beliefs guiding healthcare decisions
- Acceptable quality of life threshold
- Unacceptable conditions or treatments

#### 10. ORGAN & TISSUE DONATION
- Organ donation preferences
- Tissue donation preferences
- Donation restrictions (if any)

#### 11. FUNERAL & BURIAL WISHES
- Funeral preferences (traditional, cremation, etc.)
- Burial location
- Memorial service preferences
- Funeral cost limitations

#### 12. ATTORNEY DUTIES & RESTRICTIONS
- Duty to follow grantor's wishes
- Duty to act in grantor's best interest
- Duty to consult healthcare providers
- Restrictions on decisions

#### 13. EXECUTION DETAILS
- Date of POA signing
- Grantor signature
- Witness signatures (2 required)
- Notarization (recommended)

## DOCUMENT GENERATION REQUIREMENTS

### Will Document Structure
1. **Opening Clause** - Identification and revocation of prior wills
2. **Family Clause** - Declaration of family status
3. **Appointment of Executor** - Executor powers and duties
4. **Appointment of Guardians** - For minor children
5. **Specific Bequests** - Specific gifts to specific people
6. **Residual Estate Distribution** - Distribution of remaining assets
7. **Trust Provisions** - Any testamentary trusts
8. **Powers Clause** - Executor powers
9. **Tax Clause** - Tax payment instructions
10. **Execution Clause** - Signature blocks for testator and witnesses
11. **Attestation Clause** - Witness declarations

### POA for Property Document Structure
1. **Opening Clause** - Identification and appointment of attorney
2. **Powers Granted** - Specific powers and limitations
3. **Effective Date** - When POA becomes effective
4. **Attorney Duties** - Fiduciary duties and restrictions
5. **Compensation** - Attorney compensation terms
6. **Execution Clause** - Signature blocks
7. **Notarization** - Notary acknowledgment

### POA for Personal Care Document Structure
1. **Opening Clause** - Identification and appointment of attorney
2. **Healthcare Authority** - Scope of healthcare decisions
3. **Life-Sustaining Treatment** - Specific instructions
4. **Mental Health Treatment** - Psychiatric care preferences
5. **Palliative Care** - End-of-life preferences
6. **Personal Care Preferences** - Daily living arrangements
7. **Core Values** - Guiding principles for decisions
8. **Execution Clause** - Signature blocks
9. **Notarization** - Notary acknowledgment

## COMPLIANCE & VALIDATION REQUIREMENTS

### Will Compliance Checklist
- [ ] Testator has testamentary capacity
- [ ] Will is in writing
- [ ] Will is signed by testator
- [ ] Signature made with testamentary intent
- [ ] Two witnesses present at signing
- [ ] Witnesses not beneficiaries or spouses of beneficiaries
- [ ] Each witness acknowledges testator's signature
- [ ] Witnesses sign in presence of testator and each other
- [ ] No undue influence
- [ ] No fraud or forgery
- [ ] Complies with SLRA Section 4

### POA Compliance Checklist
- [ ] Grantor has capacity to execute POA
- [ ] POA in writing
- [ ] Signed by grantor
- [ ] Two witnesses (not beneficiaries)
- [ ] Witnesses sign in grantor's presence
- [ ] Complies with Substitute Decisions Act
- [ ] Powers clearly defined
- [ ] Effective date specified
- [ ] Attorney duties clearly stated

## COMMON WILL & POA ISSUES TO AVOID

### Will Issues
1. **Ambiguous language** - Unclear beneficiary designations
2. **Missing contingencies** - No alternate beneficiaries
3. **Unequal treatment** - Potential for family disputes
4. **Inadequate guardianship** - Unclear guardian duties
5. **Tax inefficiency** - Unnecessary probate fees
6. **Formal defects** - Witness issues, signature problems
7. **Undue influence** - Appearance of coercion
8. **Outdated provisions** - Not reflecting current circumstances

### POA Issues
1. **Overly broad powers** - Attorney has too much authority
2. **Unclear triggering events** - When POA becomes effective
3. **Missing successor attorneys** - No backup if primary unavailable
4. **Inadequate safeguards** - No accounting requirements
5. **Conflict of interest** - Attorney benefits from decisions
6. **Lack of communication** - Grantor hasn't discussed wishes with attorney
7. **Outdated document** - Not reflecting current circumstances

## BEST PRACTICES FOR DOCUMENT GENERATION

1. **Clear, plain language** - Avoid legal jargon where possible
2. **Specific identifications** - Full legal names, addresses, dates of birth
3. **Comprehensive asset listing** - All assets clearly identified
4. **Contingency planning** - Multiple levels of succession
5. **Tax optimization** - Minimize probate fees and taxes
6. **Professional formatting** - Proper legal document structure
7. **Compliance verification** - Ensure all legal requirements met
8. **Accessibility** - Easy for executors/attorneys to understand and implement
9. **Regular updates** - Recommend review every 3-5 years
10. **Secure storage** - Clear instructions on document location and access
